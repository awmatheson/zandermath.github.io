Ruby on Railsclose check it out

PHP, Asterisk and Twilio - Voice automated IVR to demonstrate the company's NLP software

Natural Language Processingclose

The company I worked for specialized in Natural Language Processing software. The audio excerpt is from a demonstration I made to showcase the software in a call center application. I used the open source Asterisk software as a digital Prvate Branch Exchange (PBX) to handle the connection to the Public Switched Telephony Network (PSTN). I used Twilio to provide an incoming number and pass the caller to the PBX. PHP was used as the language to develop the dialog manager because it allows for easy interaction with the Asterisk Gateway Interface. The dialog manager connects the user to various external tools, such as the OpenWeatherMap API to provide location based weather and the Eventbrite API for location based events.

Natural Language Slackbot - A text based interface that allows slack users to interact with other apps directly in slack

Slackbotclose check it out

*This project is still in its early stages* Due to the insane increase in popularity of Slack, I thought that it would be a fun project to try and make a slack bot. I have only just started this project and have been playing around with using webhooks and the Real Time Messaging API. The idea of the project is to build a bot that people can use to greatly increase their workflow by allowing users to interact with CRMs, ERPs, search engines and other services in a natural way, without leaving Slack.

Volunteer Consulting - Social impact and marketing consulting for a Pay-As-You-Go (PAYG) solar company Azuri

Volunteer Consultingclose

The primary lighting source for rural African families are Kerosene lamps, which are dangerous, dirty and not very effective. Azuri's PAYG solar allows families to pay a small upfront fee and then a recurring weekly fee to purchase a solar system. As part of the organization Planete d'Entrepreneurs, I worked with Azuri to understand their business and then traveled to Kenya to assess the social impact that their product had on the families. Along with another volunteer, we developed metrics to quantitatively assess the impact and also to assess the effectiveness of the distribution network used to sell and support their product. We presented our findings which were ultimately used by the company to secure further financing and increase awareness of the company's mission.
